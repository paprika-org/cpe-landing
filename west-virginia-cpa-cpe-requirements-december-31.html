Understanding West Virginia's Three Compliance Dates

West Virginia CPAs must navigate three separate annual deadlines — an unusual structure compared to most states that combine CPE completion and reporting in one step.

December 31 CPE completion
All hours must be earned by this date
January 31 CPE reporting
Submit report to WV Board (boa.wv.gov)
June 30 License renewal
Pay $85 renewal fee to maintain CPA

Courses completed on January 1 or later count toward the next year's CPE — not the year just ended. Report all prior-year CPE by January 31 to avoid the $150 late filing surcharge.

Ethics CPE in West Virginia

West Virginia requires 4 hours of ethics CPE within each rolling three-year period. Unlike Wyoming (which requires a Board-approved WY-specific ethics course) or many states that mandate a particular course, West Virginia accepts any qualifying ethics content from a qualified provider.

Frequently Asked Questions — West Virginia CPA CPE

How many CPE hours do West Virginia CPAs need?
West Virginia CPAs must complete 120 hours of CPE within any rolling three-year period, with a minimum of 20 hours in each individual calendar year. Within the 120 hours, 4 must be in ethics. There is no A&A mandate and no self-study cap. The WV Board examines CPE across the current calendar year plus the two prior years at each January 31 reporting deadline.
When is the West Virginia CPA CPE deadline?
West Virginia has three separate annual deadlines: (1) December 31 — all CPE hours must be completed by this date; (2) January 31 — CPE must be reported to the WV Board of Accountancy and any extension requests submitted; (3) June 30 — annual CPA license renewal. Missing any one of these three dates triggers separate fees and compliance consequences.
Do I need a West Virginia-specific ethics course?
No. Unlike Wyoming (which requires a Board-approved WY-specific ethics course) or some other states, West Virginia accepts any qualifying ethics content from a NASBA-registered provider or other Board-qualified sponsor. Ethics content must cover ethical reasoning, state statutes/rules, or standards of professional conduct. AICPA ethics courses, other states' ethics courses, WVSCPA offerings, and government agency ethics courses all qualify. No specific WV ethics course is mandated.
What happens if I skip CPE in one year?
You will fail the 20-hour annual minimum for that year, even if your rolling 3-year total equals 120 hours. West Virginia's annual minimum is a hard floor — it cannot be made up retroactively. If you completed 60 hours in each of 2024 and 2025 but zero in 2026, your rolling total is 120 (passes) but 2026's annual minimum is 0 (fails). You would be placed in CPE noncompliance and ineligible for license renewal until the deficiency is resolved.
Can I carry over excess CPE hours in West Virginia?
No. West Virginia does not provide for carryover of excess CPE hours. The rolling three-year window only counts hours within the current and two prior calendar years. Hours completed outside the active window — for example, hours from three or more years ago — cannot be applied to any current or future reporting period. Unlike Utah (which allows 40 hours of carryover) or some biennial states, WV's rolling window is the only framework used.
Is there a self-study cap in West Virginia?
No. West Virginia does not impose a percentage cap on self-study CPE hours. You may complete all 120 required hours via self-study courses from NASBA-registered providers or other Board-qualified sponsors. This makes West Virginia one of the more permissive states for self-study delivery. Compare to states like Kentucky (50% self-study cap = 60hr max) or Ohio (50% cap). The only delivery format rules that apply in WV are those of the individual CPE provider.
How do I request a CPE extension in West Virginia?
Submit an extension request to the WV Board of Accountancy by January 31. The fee is $75 if submitted by January 31, or $225 ($75 + $150 late surcharge) if submitted after January 31. The Board will not grant extensions past June 15, in order to preserve time for the June 30 license renewal. If approved, the extension typically runs until approximately June 1. Hardship waivers (for accident, illness, or military service) are submitted in writing and require no fee; the Board responds within 15 days.
What are the CPE rules for new West Virginia CPA licensees?
New licensees follow a graduated schedule: the year of initial licensure is CPE-exempt (but you must file an exempt-status form by January 31). Your first full calendar year requires 40 hours minimum. Your second full year requires 20 hours. Your third full year onward subjects you to the full 120-hour rolling 3-year requirement with 4 ethics hours. This graduated approach gives new licensees time to build up their CPE cadence without immediately facing the rolling-window calculation.
When is the West Virginia CPA license renewal deadline?
West Virginia CPA licenses must be renewed annually by June 30. The renewal fee is $85. A grace period runs July 1–31 with an additional $50 late fee ($135 total). After July 31, a Reinstatement of License application is required, with reinstatement fees of $75/month (capped at $445) in addition to the standard $85 renewal fee. Note that the June 30 renewal is a separate deadline from the January 31 CPE reporting deadline — both must be met each year.
How long do I need to keep CPE records in West Virginia?
West Virginia requires CPAs to retain CPE documentation — certificates of completion, transcripts, and attendance records — for a minimum of 4 calendar years. The Board conducts random CPE audits and may request supporting documentation for any period within that retention window. Separately, the Board retains its own CPE waiver files for a minimum of 10 years. Best practice: keep all CPE certificates permanently in an organized digital archive.
Does West Virginia require A&A CPE hours?
No. The WV Board of Accountancy does not impose a mandatory accounting and auditing (A&A) hour requirement. This differentiates West Virginia from states like Indiana (12 hours A&A for attest practitioners in the current triennial) or New Jersey (specific A&A requirements). West Virginia CPAs who perform audit, review, or attest services are expected to maintain professional competency through voluntary CPE, but there is no regulatory floor for A&A hours.
How does West Virginia compare to neighboring states?
West Virginia (120hr rolling 3-yr, 20hr/yr mandatory annual floor, Dec 31 / Jan 31 / Jun 30 three dates, no A&A, no self-study cap) differs from neighbors: Virginia has no annual minimum and only 2hr ethics per year; Kentucky is biennial (odd years only), 120hr rolling, no annual min, 50% self-study cap; Ohio is also biennial odd years, no annual min, 50% cap; Pennsylvania is 80hr biennial (odd years), 20hr/yr min (shared with WV), 24hr A&A for attest. WV's mandatory 20hr/year and three-date structure are its most distinctive features.

Step-by-Step: How to Meet West Virginia CPA CPE Requirements

  1. Mark your calendar with all three dates: December 31 (CPE completion), January 31 (reporting), June 30 (renewal).
  2. Plan 20+ hours per year: Do not defer — the annual minimum cannot be made up later.
  3. Track your rolling 3-year total: Count hours from this year plus the two prior years. Aim for 120+.
  4. Include 4 ethics hours: Any NASBA-registered ethics provider is acceptable. No WV-specific course required.
  5. Choose Board-qualified providers: NASBA Registry membership is sufficient — WV has no separate pre-approval list.
  6. Complete all CPE by December 31: Hours earned after December 31 count toward next year, not the current year.
  7. Report CPE by January 31: Submit your report at boa.wv.gov. If you need more time, file for an extension (and pay $75) by January 31.
  8. Retain certificates for 4 years: Keep digital copies of all CPE certificates in case of a Board audit.
  9. Renew license by June 30: Pay the $85 renewal fee at boa.wv.gov. Do not wait for a renewal notice.
