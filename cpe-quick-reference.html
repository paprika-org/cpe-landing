Frequently Asked Questions

Which states have the December 31, 2026 triennial deadline?

New Jersey and Indiana have fixed triennial cycles ending December 31, 2026. New Jersey has approximately 20,500 CPAs in this cohort. Virginia and Washington also have December 31 triennial deadlines, but individual renewal dates vary by CPA. Ohio and New York use rolling triennial windows — your deadline depends on your license date, not a fixed cohort year. Check your individual state board record to confirm your specific deadline.

Which December 31 states allow unlimited self-study CPE?

Colorado and Washington are the notable exceptions — both allow 100% of CPE hours to be self-study with no cap. Most other December 31 states cap self-study at 50% of total hours. This makes CO and WA particularly CPA-friendly for on-demand CPE completion. Always verify the current rule with your state board as policies can change.

Do I need state-specific ethics courses or will any ethics CPE count?

It depends on your state. States with specific ethics requirements: Virginia (must cite VBOA regulations and Title 54.1 Code of Virginia), Texas (must be TSBPA-approved — NASBA ethics don't count), California (must be CA-specific). States where standard NASBA-approved ethics count: New Jersey, Indiana, Colorado, North Carolina, Ohio, and most others. When in doubt, check your state board's approved provider list.

Is Pennsylvania due December 31, 2026?

No. Pennsylvania's biennial CPE deadline falls on December 31 of odd years (2025, 2027, 2029…). 2026 is not a Pennsylvania deadline year. Pennsylvania CPAs who completed their 2025 deadline are not due again until December 31, 2027.

Does New York have the same December 31 deadline as New Jersey?

No. New York uses a rolling triennial system — each CPA has their own 3-year CPE reporting cycle that ends December 31 of the third year, but the specific year depends on your license issue date. Unlike New Jersey and Indiana (fixed cohort years), there is no single year when all New York CPAs are due simultaneously. You need to check your individual NYSED license record to determine your specific triennial end date.
