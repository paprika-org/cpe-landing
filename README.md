## What This Is

A comprehensive reference for Certified Public Accountants (CPAs) tracking their **Continuing Professional Education (CPE)** requirements across all U.S. states.

Each state guide covers:
- **Total hours required** (per period)
- **Deadline date** (December 31, June 30, or other)
- **Ethics hour requirement** (state-specific or NASBA-accepted)
- **Self-study limits** (if any)
- **Annual minimums** (if applicable)
- **Carryover rules**
- **New licensee proration** tables
- **Multi-state comparison** for CPAs holding licenses in multiple states
- **Official regulator contact and portal links**

All pages include FAQ, HowTo, and Article schema markup for AI/LLM discoverability.

## Key Facts for CPAs

**What is CPE?** Continuing Professional Education (CPE) is mandatory training CPAs must complete to maintain their CPA license. Requirements are set by each state's Board of Accountancy and administered through NASBA (National Association of State Boards of Accountancy).

**How many CPE hours do CPAs need?** Most states require **80 hours every 2 years** or **120 hours every 3 years**, with at least **4 hours of ethics** training per period.

**When is the CPE deadline?** The most common deadline is **December 31** of the renewal year. States with June 30 deadlines include Florida, Illinois, Texas, and others. A few states use birth-month or license-issuance rolling periods.

**What counts as CPE?** Live seminars, webinars, self-study courses, college courses, and teaching/authoring activities typically count. Each state has specific rules on self-study limits and subject matter requirements.

**What is the ethics CPE requirement?** Nearly all states require **2–4 hours of ethics CPE** per renewal period. Some states require a state-specific ethics course; others accept any NASBA-registered ethics program.

**What happens if you miss the CPE deadline?** Penalties vary by state but typically include: a late fee, a grace period (30–90 days), reinstatement requirements if the license lapses, and in some cases penalty CPE (completing additional hours as a condition of reinstatement).
